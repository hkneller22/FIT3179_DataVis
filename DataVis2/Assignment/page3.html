Outcomes

- Individuals with higher levels of education, such as bachelor's, master's, and postgraduate degrees, generally earn higher incomes compared to those with only secondary education. This can be observed by the clustering of large circles associated with any qualification in the left figure. Conversely, large circles are associated with a lower income for peoples with no non-school qualification.


- The bottom left figure highlights the difference in average weekly income between sexes. Whether considering either mean or median, it can be observed that there is an unnaceptably large pay gap.


- Each pie chart below displays the proportion of peoples in each employment category, be that full-time, part-time, unemployed or not-in-labour-market. The top chart presents the proportions for people with atleast one non-school qualification, whilst the other presents proportions for people only with secondary education. Interestingly, nearly half of all people with no non-school qualifications are not in the labour force - i.e. retired, special needs individuals or caregivers.
